Getting real CFD (channel losses & mixing)

Channel pressure losses from your Onshape CAD — SimScale workflow (free, ~1 evening):
  1. In Onshape: create the fluid volume (Boolean: enclose the internal passages, subtract the metal). Export as STEP.
  2. Make a free account at simscale.com → new project → upload the STEP.
  3. Analysis: Incompressible. Fluid: water as an IPA stand-in (or add IPA: ρ 786 kg/m³, μ 2.4 mPa·s). For the N₂O side, run liquid at saturated density (~770 kg/m³ @ 68 °F) — this ignores flashing, so treat the loss as a lower bound.
  4. Boundary conditions: pressure inlet at the port (your feed pressure), pressure outlet at the orifice exits (chamber pressure), no-slip walls.
  5. Mesh: standard, with refinement on small channels (aim ≥ 8–10 cells across the narrowest passage).
  6. Result: mass flow at the given ΔP → your effective CdA and channel K. Feed those numbers into the fields above.
Mixing / combustion (why thrust dropped with bigger holes): reacting CFD is genuinely hard — before burning compute time on it, check the 0-D warnings above: if ΔP/Pc fell below ~15 %, atomization degrades and η drops, which this model shows directly. For real combustion simulation, talk to the Astro dept about ANSYS Fluent / Rocket Propulsion Analysis (RPA) licenses and run NASA CEA to replace this page's approximate c* table with exact values.
⚠ Educational model. N₂O properties and CEA values are approximations; the two-phase injector model is a derated SPI (not full Dyer/HEM); blowdown assumes equilibrium saturation. It will catch systems-level mistakes (ΔP margin, O/F drift, feed coupling) but is not a substitute for cold-flow, water-flow, and incremental hot-fire testing. N₂O is an energetic oxidizer — follow your range safety procedures.
